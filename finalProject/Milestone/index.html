Progress So Far

As of now, we have accomplished the functionality to split our cloth into two. This involves dividing the cloth into two and recreating springs to ensure that the cloths are two separate entities. In addition to that, we have implemented the functionality for cloths to be able to detect each other and handle collisions amongst one another.

Preliminary results

Below shows us testing with different textures and displacements to see how to make sure that our pasta looks realistic and mimics the same looks and qualities. Overall, we are satisfied with the current pastas we have and hope to make them look more realistic.

Reflection

With the more cloths that we add, there is a concern of adding on time complexity and making the simulations slower. We have to be aware of how adding cloths makes a difference and to ensure some processes aren’t run multiple times such as self collisions and object collisions. We also need to do some minor tweaks on self-collision to prevent cloths from going through one another and random movements when a cloth should be stable.

Next Steps

We need to continue working on making the noodles more realistic. This means finding the right texture to put on the noodles and the bumps and displacements. Moreover, we want to make the noodles 3D. This will add another dimension to the pasta noodles and involve new interactions.

The overall simulation also has to be built to simulate a pasta factory. We need to create the 3D items such as a cutting machine, conveyor belt, bowl, and cooking machine on Blender.
